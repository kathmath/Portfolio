ABOUT

Hi, I'm Kathleen, a UX Designer living in San Francisco. I love figuring out how things work and what makes people tick. I dig getting to the heart of problems and developing solutions that solve problems for people and businesses. I approach design with a systems perspective informed by my years of experience as a mechanical system designer. I'm an engineer at heart and am driven to figure out how things work to understand the possibilities and contraints of problems.

Aside from the normal rote design process: Problem Definition, Research, Ideation, Design & Prototyping, Testing, Rinse and Repeat, here are a few of my thoughts on process.

Getting a real handle on design intent and objectives. Spending the time up front to research and understand user and business goals and how they come together is of the utmost importance. There's no use in a beautiful, elegant design no one has any use for. Clearly defining intent and using it as a touchstone in design decisions is key. Reevaluating intent as the project unfolds is just as important, being willing to set aside a direction that is no longer working can be painful but is better in the long run.

I love to prototype early and often. Doing rough prototypes, especially at the beginning, makes it easier to change directions and get things tested before spending a lot of time trying to perfect them. When I was starting out as a designer and got frustrated I had to start over on a design I'd been working on for months due to client feedback. My supervisor said, "Relax it's only lines on paper right now, imagine if they'd waited until building started." I really took that to heart and it makes me embrace change and iteration in the design process.

Make the whole project team part of the design process. Getting everyone involved not only creates better buy in it allows for interesting perspectives. Additionally, understanding the points of view of team members and what is important to them makes everything run more smoothly and happily. I see part of my role as getting everyone to understand and care about the users and be on the same page.

Check out my résumé or my blog where share my thoughts on design or whatever happens to be on my mind.
